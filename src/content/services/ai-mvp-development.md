Most startups fail because they spend too much time polishing features before validating whether the product solves a real problem.

AI products amplify this problem. Founders often focus on the model itself instead of the workflow around it — onboarding, usability, response quality, and long-term value.

A strong AI MVP isn't just a chatbot connected to an API. It's a focused product designed to test one important assumption quickly, gather feedback from real users, and evolve based on evidence instead of guesses.

I build AI MVPs with scalability in mind from the beginning — fast enough for validation, but structured enough to grow into a real product if the idea proves itself.
