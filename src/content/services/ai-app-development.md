Most AI apps fail because they focus on the technology instead of the actual product experience.

Users don't care which model powers the app. They care whether the product saves time, teaches something useful, or solves a real problem.

That's why the focus is not just on AI integration itself, but on building products where AI improves the workflow naturally — without making the interface confusing or overcomplicated.

I work mainly with startups, educational products, and custom internal tools where speed, flexibility, and practical UX matter more than enterprise complexity.

Whether it's an AI-powered educational platform, a productivity tool, or an MVP for testing a new idea, the goal stays the same: build something useful, scalable, and understandable for real users.
